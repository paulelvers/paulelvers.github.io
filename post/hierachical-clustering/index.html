Hierachical clustering of musical taste

@ · Aug 20, 2015 · 2 min read

Clustering is an important tool in data science that allows detecting similarities of objects in a data set. It is not one algorithm but describes a family of algorithms that seeks to identify objects that closely resemble each other. From a given set of features, a measure of proximity for each object of a data set is derived. Objects are then clustered based on the distance measure. The analysis yields “clusters” (groups or types of things) that share a unique set of features and distinguish the objects from those in other clusters.

In my research I was interested in types of music preference. Based on survey data (n=1003) preference ratings for musical styles were obtained. In to identify clusters of music listeniners that share a particular musical taste I applied hierachical clustering. The features I used for the analysis were preference ratings of musical taste on five preference dimensions “Hard Rock”, “Jazz”,“Rock”,“Pop”,“Classical”.

It was decided to use a distance matrix with a Euclidean distance measure. Figure 1 shows a cluster-dendrogram of the hierarchical agglomeration, which starts at the bottom with each case as a cluster until the top where all cases are merged into one cluster. The optimal number of clusters was determined by the maximal increase in cluster height, a criterion described by Everitt (1974). This resulted in a three-cluster-solution, as indicated by the red lines in the plot in Figure 1.
